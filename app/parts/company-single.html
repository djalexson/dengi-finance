  • A promise of a return on investment is significantly higher than that offered by banks.

  • Non-transparent mechanisms for investing funds in a company offering to invest through it.

  • A promise to pay the interest out of funds received from new participants.

  • No license to operate in the financial market, as well as the lack of information about the company in the registers of regulators
